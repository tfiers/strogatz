\chapter{Aero-elastic galloping}

From Newton's second law and the definitions of linear acceleration $\ddot{y}$ and speed $\dot{y}$, the motion of the bridge element is described by
%
\begin{align*}
\dot{x} &= - \qty(\rho\ V^2\ a\ C(x/V)\ /2  +  r x  +  k y) / m \\
\dot{y} &= x
\end{align*}
%
with $m, \rho, r, k, a, $ and $C(\alpha)$ as given.


\Cref{fig:eigencup} shows the eigenvalues of Jacobian at the fixed point (at the origin) as a function of $V$. At $V = V_C = 809.37...$ the eigenvalues land on the real axis, and the system undergoes a bifurcation. $V_C$ was determined numerically as the solution to $4 \Delta = \tau^2$ (see \cref{sec:predator}). Additionally, the trace of the Jacobian is $\tau = -0.023475.. V âˆ’ 1$. For every $V \geq 0$, $\tau < 0$. Hence, the fixed point is locally never an unstable spiral or node, but rather a stable spiral or node. Based on this linear analysis, we can classify the stability of the fixed point as in \cref{tab:fp_class}.
